About Me

My name is Catherine and I am a mid-career changer who has accrued diverse experience in a variety of roles, including working for the picture desk of a national newspaper, in marketing for a chain of restaurants, for myself as a freelance translator, and more recently, in the voluntary sector as both an employee and a volunteer.

Since the pandemic, WFH has meant sharing a workspace with a software engineer, and this has given me an invaluable insight into the profession and its processes - stand-ups, sprints, retros etc. As someone who is motivated by effective collaboration, high performance, and continuous learning and improvement, my interest was piqued. However, having thought of myself (reductively!) as a "humanities person" all my adult life, I didn't believe at the time that a stem career could really be for me.

A couple of years later and to meet the requirements of a job vacancy which asked for some experience of HTML and JavaScript, I turned to Codecademy and found to my surprise that I very much enjoyed it. Around the same time, some personality testing via my university's careers service also suggested the field would be a very good fit for me. All roads now seemed to point to software engineering, and so I took the plunge and enrolled on General Assembly's immersive bootcamp. It was fantastic to be back in a learning environment and to be really challenged by something totally new, and I am looking forward to starting out in the field, knowing I will be expected to continue to learn and improve throughout my career.

I hope to secure a full-time position working with super fun people who are super serious about what they do, where I will be supported and encouraged to understand my new craft inside out. As a colleague, I think I'm adept at asking the right questions and at looking ahead to forestall problems. Being a linguist, I'm a clear, adaptable communicator. With a background in written translation, I pride myself on (and bore other people with) my attention to detail.

I am particularly interested in backend development, and I subscribe to the Talk Python to Me podcast. Having heard Eric Matthes on the show, I have also begun his Python Crash Course book. I enjoy the JS Party and CoRecursive podcasts as well, and particularly liked this recent episode of the latter. With the bootcamp successfully completed, I am embracing the opportunity to practise what I've learned so far and to broaden/deepen my understanding of the discipline.

I am always happy and motivated to learn independently; one of my main interests is languages, and having studied French and Spanish as my degree, I have also taught myself Italian, and dabbled with other languages too. I work daily to maintain and improve these skills using a variety of resources, from Duolingo (of course) to podcasts, YouTube, conversation "exchanges" with learners of English, and Netflix, to name a few. I also love to read in all my languages, and am a member of my university's alumni book group.

Using DOM manipulation, I built a grid-based browser version of 80s arcade game Frogger in HTML, CSS and JavaScript across 6 business days (8 in total). My main challenges included animating the obstacles and detecting collisions with my player character. My stretch goals included a high score display and increasing the difficulty as the game progresses.
